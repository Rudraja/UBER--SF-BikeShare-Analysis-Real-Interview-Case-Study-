# 🚲 San Francisco Bike Sharing Analysis

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes bike-sharing data in San Francisco to understand user behavior, demand patterns, and operational efficiency.

The goal is to identify key trends and provide actionable insights to improve system utilization, reliability, and growth.

---

## 📊 Key Insights

- ~87% of rides occur on weekdays → strong commute-driven usage  
- Peak demand at **8 AM and 5 PM** → clear work-hour patterns  
- ~84% of users are **subscribers**, forming a stable core user base  
- Top 5 stations contribute ~30% of total traffic → demand is highly concentrated  
- Strong directional imbalance across stations → requires rebalancing  
- Majority of trips are **short (<20 minutes)** → system optimized for quick rides  

---

## 🧠 Business Recommendations

### 🚀 Growth Opportunities
- Target **weekend and leisure users** through promotions  
- Convert casual users into subscribers  
- Introduce **day passes** and tourist-focused offerings  

### ⚙️ Operational Improvements
- Ensure bike availability at high-demand stations  
- Implement **rebalancing strategies** based on demand patterns  
- Relocate underutilized bikes and stations  

### 🔧 Efficiency Enhancements
- Focus maintenance on high-usage bikes  
- Investigate long-duration trips for potential inefficiencies
